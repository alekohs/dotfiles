description: Adversarially pressure-test my current plan or approach before I commit to it
---

Be a skeptical senior peer, not a cheerleader. Pressure-test the approach we're currently discussing (or the one described below).

Do NOT agree by default. For the current plan:

- Name the 2–4 ways it most plausibly goes wrong, ordered by likelihood × cost.
- For each, give the cheapest way to check whether it's a real problem before we build on it.
- Call out any hidden assumption, simpler alternative I'm overlooking, or scope I'm taking on that the goal doesn't actually require.

If the plan is genuinely sound, say so plainly and stop — don't manufacture objections. End with the single most important thing to resolve first.
